Justine Zhu

I knew as a kid I always wanted to be a journalist ever since I was a kid. Writing stories about the world always fascinated me, but I never had the confidence that I would succeed, that is until I signed up to shadow a mentor at WeNetwork. From my experiences shadowing a real journalist, I realized: 'Hey, this is pretty doable. I can do this. I'm ready for this.' From there, I honed my writing more and more throughout college and now I'm a full fledged journalist employed at a publication.

Mary Teol

WeNetwork helped provide me a pathway to succeed. I was a buisness major in college and I was looking for internships over the summer. All my friends seemed to getting one left and right, but I could never find one. In the middle of that panic, I decided to attend a networking event hosted by WeNetwork. In the middle of that event, I met the owner of a local buisness firm. We talked for a bit, and afterwards he offered me a internship, saying he respected my passion and drive. Such possibilities could've never happened without WeNetwork.

George Hanweiz

When I was 16, I had absolute no idea what I was going to do for a living. I found about WeNetwork from some of my friends, and I had nothing better to do so I signed up for a electrical engineering workshop. Little did I know how much that would change my life. Seeing all those electronics interact with other was simply a transformative moment, and from that point onwards I was absorbed into the world of engineering. Now I'm enrolled in college studying electrical engineering and learning more about my passions.

Henry Doeritz

My life was very confusing for me in high school. That all changed after WeNetwork and its workshops. I hated writing before. I thought it was such a long and tedious process, but when I decided to go to a writing workshop with one of my friends on a whim, I learned how much fun it could be. The author there taught to me have fun with it and how to immerse myself in the fantasies I would create. It was wonderful, and now I'm about to publish my very own book later this year.

Farwa Sinhal

Before WeNetwork, I didn't think I'd want to be a computer scientist at all. But I ended up browsing of the videos about computer science on this website and found it super intriguing. I researched more and more, and I made my first app a few months later. I placed it on the app store, and it was such a gratifying feeling. I'm studying computer science at college now, and it's a blast.

Han Zhang

WeNetwork gave me the courage to decide on being a professional chef. I loved cooking, but my parents were always against the idea of me becoming a chef. I went to a cooking event hosted by a chef on WeNetwork, and he and I really bonded. He told me about how he had a similar experience growing up and how he ended up going for it after finding out he really wasn't fit for accounting. His stories really motivated to take that next step in life, and I thank WeNetwork for that opportunity.
